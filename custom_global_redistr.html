If you could redistribute income at the global level, what would you do? In this question, we let you choose your preferred parameters for a redistribution of income at the world level. If you prefer to skip this question, check the box at the bottom of the page.

The worldwide redistribution of income would take the form of additional policies, taxes, and transfers, on top of existing ones, that would lower the after-tax income of the richest and increase the income of the poorest. 

Below you will find a graph of the world distribution of after-tax income and 3 sliders that vary it. The current distribution is in red, and your custom one is in green. 
The first two sliders control the proportion of people to benefit from the redistribution (those with the lowest incomes), and the proportion of people to lose out (those with the highest incomes). The third slider controls the degree of redistribution from the richest to the poorest. 
If you do not want new policies to reduce global inequality, you can set the third slider to zero. 

You need to move the sliders (by holding the mouse down on the little squares and moving to the side) to see the green curve evolve: the idea is to move the sliders until you get a green curve you are happy with.
